F2L (First 2 Layers)

F2L is the second step of the CFOP method that solves the first layer corners and the second layer edges simultaneously.

Using beginner F2L, there are usually 3 steps to each pair: bring both pieces to the top layer separated, pair them up, then insert the pair.

Note: in the pictures in this section, the blue squares on the cube are irrelevant to the step and are meant to be ignored.

F2L Tutorial

Special case: pieces need to be separated

If you see that the two F2L pieces are stuck together and aren't paired up, then the steps below won't work; you need to separate them first. Above is an example of how you can separate them. Once you have, you can pair them up using the steps above.

Step 1: Get both pieces in the top layer

After finishing the cross, the first step to solving an F2L pair is to get both pieces in the top layer. For example, if you were working on the red-green pair, you want to get the red-green-white corner piece and the red-green edge piece into the top layer. You can use right- or left-hand algorithms for this step. If you come across the case where the two pieces are stuck together, go here.

Step 2: Pair them up

Case 1: White sticker is on the side

If the white sticker of the corner piece is not on the side (is on top of the cube), go to case 2. If the white sticker is on the side of the corner piece, start by moving the corner in between the two corresponding center pieces (like you did in the beginner method). If the corner is on top, then go to that step. Use either a U or U' move to put the corner somewhere else where you can still see the white sticker.
Case 1a: Look at the colors facing up on the corner and edge. If the colors are the same, the edge needs to go next to the corner. If this case doesn't apply to you, go to case B. To move the edge to its correct spot, "hide" the corner. If the corner is on the right, hide it like in the first picture below. If it's on the left, hide it like in the second picture.
Once you've hidden the corner, use U moves to move the edge next to the corner. Then bring the corner back from where you "hid" it.
Case 1b: If the colors facing up on the two pieces are different, the edge needs to go in the slot opposite the corner. To move the edge to its correct spot, "hide" the corner as described in Case A. Then move the edge to the slot opposite the corner, and bring the corner back.

Case 2: White sticker is on the top

If the white sticker is on top (facing up), then you need to pair it up a different way. Start by aligning the edge piece with its corresponding center piece.
"Hide" the edge by moving it away. To do this, hold the two center pieces of the pair you're doing in front of you (ex. if you're doing the red-green pair, hold the red and green centers in front of you). Then move the edge away. If the edge is on the right, hide it like in the first picture below. If it's on the left, hide it like in the second picture.
Next, move the corner on top of the edge that you "hid".
Finally, undo the move that you did to "hide" the edge, and the pieces should be paired up.

Step 3: Insert the pair

There are four ways a pair may be paired up. The pieces might be touching each other, or they might not. For each of those cases, there is a right-hand and left-hand version. Below are the four ways to insert a pair based on the case you have. Look for your case and follow the steps.

Pieces touching: right version

If the pieces are touching, and they look like the first image below, then start by moving the pair to the position in the second image below (relative to the two corresponding centers). Next, bring the right side up (R). Then, connect the pair with the cross edge (U'). Finally, move the cross edge down (R'), and the pair is inserted.

Pieces touching: left-hand version

If the pieces are touching, and they look like the first image below, then start by moving the pair to the position in the second image below (relative to the two corresponding centers). Next, bring the left side up (L'). Then, connect the pair with the cross edge (U). Finally, move the cross edge down (L), and the pair is inserted.

Pieces not touching: right-hand version

If the pieces are not touching, and they look like the first image below, then align the corner with its two corresponding center pieces (just like in the beginner method), and just insert the corner with a right-hand algorithm (R U R' U', although the last U' is unnecessary)! This will insert both the corner and the edge.

Pieces not touching: left-hand version

If the pieces are not touching, and they look like the first image below, then align the corner with its two corresponding center pieces (just like in the beginner method), and just insert the corner with a left-hand algorithm (L' U' L U, although the last U is unnecessary)! This will insert both the corner and the edge.
